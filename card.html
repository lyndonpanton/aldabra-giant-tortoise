Aldabra Giant Tortoise

They are regard as the elepant of their habitats, being the most formiable creature and making paths of other animals by going on their daily lives, pushing large obstacles and causing nutrition to fall from trees by merely passing by due to their large weight.

  • Scientific Name: Aldabrachelys Gigantea
  • Average Weight: : 250kg
  • Average Lifespan: 80 - 120 years
  • Habitat: Scrubs, Mangrove swamps, Coastal dunes

The reptile has a tan dome shaped tail and stumped like legs to support its weight as well as large claws to aid with digging, they get their name from the place thought to be where they originated from, Aldabra Island. They are primarily herbivores, feeding on mostly grass, leaves and woody plant stem though they may occasionaly feed on small invertebrates and even the carcasses of other tortoises.
